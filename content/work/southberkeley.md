A proposal for the South Berkeley Specific Plan, presented to the City of Berkeley on behalf of a team in Professor Elizabeth Macdonald's Plan Preparation Studio at UC Berkeley College of Environmental Design.

**My Role:** Analysis, design strategy, and 3D site modeling

The southernmost point of Adeline Street's public right-of-way in Berkeley is wider than a football field. My proposal to the city included the transformation of this dramatic surplus of vehicle space into entirely new city blocks flanked by two smaller streets on either side, restoring human-scaled proportions to the area.

**Project Outcomes:**
- Approximately 290 housing units
- 40 retail spaces
- Financial benefit to Berkeley through parcel sales and property tax revenue
- Converted maintenance expense into net positive for the city

Beyond the infill-block concept, my contributions to the larger proposal included the creation of a 3D model of South Berkeley, which allowed our team vastly greater flexibility in testing competing design concepts.
