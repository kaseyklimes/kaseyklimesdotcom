One of the great things about living in New York is that you can decide to go do something completely out of character and do it with the best people in the world at that thing and nobody has to know, like living a secret second life.

I took Shannon O'Neill's improv 101 class at the Upright Citizens Brigade as an exercise in cognitive agility and vulnerability, precisely because I knew it'd make me incredibly uncomfortable. While my classmates had IMDB pages and standup comedy careers, I was a complete beginner with zero intention of becoming a performer. For me the whole thing was an experiment.

As experiments go, it was wildly generative. I now think everyone should take an improv class.
